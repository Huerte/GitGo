# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want to build a safe, welcoming, and inclusive community. We pledge to make participation in our project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of background, identity, or level of experience.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy environment and to respect everyone who contributes to this project.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:
* Using welcoming and inclusive language.
* Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences.
* Gracefully accepting constructive criticism.
* Focusing on what is best for the overall project.
* Showing empathy towards other community members.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:
* The use of sexualized language or imagery.
* Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal attacks.
* Public or private harassment.
* Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
* Dismissing or mocking someone for their English proficiency or technical skill level.
* Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including the repository, issues, pull requests, and discussions. It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces, such as on social media or at events.

## Enforcement

The project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior. They will review all issues, comments, and contributions to ensure they align with this code.

For minor or first-time infractions, maintainers will issue a warning and explain how the behavior must change. For serious or repeated issues, maintainers reserve the right to edit or remove comments, commits, and issues, and may temporarily or permanently ban the individual involved.

### Reporting Incidents

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns, please report it directly to the maintainers. Please do not open a public issue to report code of conduct violations.
